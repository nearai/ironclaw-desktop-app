# IronClaw Desktop — Elite-Readiness Audit (2026-06-14)
Exhaustive hostile design + functionality audit of the committed static UI (nearai/main @ 96b1bb4) against the Design Laws. 106 agents, every shipped JS file read, each finding adversarially verified by an independent skeptic. **79 confirmed**, 7 refuted.
## Verdict
> No — IronClaw Desktop is strong but not yet elite, and the gap is not cosmetic. The token system, dense-bordered design language, sacred-gate disclosure structure, and the breadth of hardened dialogs (modal.js, command-palette, configure-modal) are genuinely good and show real craft. But the audit surfaces a tier of defects that directly violate the product's own stated laws ("no fake readiness", "no dead affordances", sacred gates must be reliable), and they are confirmed in the committed source — not theoretical.
> 
> The honest assessment: this ships with five load-bearing failures. (1) The approval gate's Approve/Deny resolution is a bare un-awaited promise with no try/catch (useChat.js:553-577) — a failed POST leaves a money/delete gate silently stuck and throws unhandled. That is the single most important surface in the product and its recovery path is unguarded. (2) The desktop SSE transport never reconnects on clean stream-end (FetchEventStream.read breaks on `done` and start() returns without dispatching onerror) — chat goes silently dead behind a green badge. (3) The failed-send "Retry" button is wired to a literal no-op (retryMessage: noop). (4) Markdown links have no target/rel and no Tauri shell-open interceptor — a link click navigates the whole WKWebView off the app shell with no back button. (5) The Settings Users "Add user" form resolves a {success:false} stub as success — textbook fake-readiness, ungated and untested.
> 
> What is genuinely missing structurally: the gate stack is partly theater. The bundle-freshness check is mtime-only against a 2-line bootstrap (proven bypassable by deep-source edits), the i18n "completeness" gate freezes 308 missing keys/locale as an accepted baseline — meaning the approval gate, auth gate, and onboarding ship untranslated in all 10 non-English languages while the picker advertises 11 — and the gate-enforcement smoke only asserts English. The token-fidelity lint cannot see alias-with-opacity classes that render as no-ops, so 24 such classes ship as invisible inputs/borders. And Arabic is advertised + auto-detected but `dir=rtl` is never set, so the entire RTL UI renders mirrored-wrong. These are the difference between "looks elite in a demo" and "is elite under the contract it claims." Fix the five functional failures and the three gate holes first; the rest is polish that the now-honest gates will keep honest.
